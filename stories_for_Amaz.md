# Behavioral Questions and All Complex Situations in Life 
### Highly challenging, but did not give up
**Situation 1:** Switching from a school in the local language to an English medium, where it was difficult to follow the classes taught in English. Sometimes, I felt I would be better off going back to the old school and continuing in the local language. However, I did not do that. Despite initial failures and criticism from some faculty members, when I request them to translate a few questions in the classroom, they do not do it. Rather, focused on learning English from everyone who spoke, where I started to remember certain new words and how they made up sentences with them. Slowly started giving speeches and engaging in classroom talks. Many mistakes, but eventually I got rid of the fear of making mistakes first, then corrected them. Compared to the earlier version, my current version is far better. Learning is not giving up on problems that seem impossible and difficult. In reality, it is not impossible; it just needs an attempt.
